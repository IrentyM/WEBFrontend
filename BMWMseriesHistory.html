7 Milestones from 50 Years of BMW M History

Introduction

The M Series by BMW has become iconic over the last 50 years. From its beginnings in motorsport to its evolution into a luxury performance brand, it continues to shape the automotive world.

Impact

In the world of cars, the BMW M Series stands out as the pinnacle of engineering and design. Here are seven key milestones that defined the history of this legendary series.

BMW 3.0 CSL (E9) 1971

The first BMW M car, developed with Alpina, featured a 3.2L inline-six producing 206 hp in its final 1973 form. Weighing just 1,270 kg, the lightweight “Coupé Sport Leichtbau” earned the nickname “Batmobile” with its striking design and impressive performance. Only 167 units were made.

BMW M1 (E26) 1978

Designed by Giorgio Giugiaro, the BMW M1 combined a 3.5L four-valve engine and racetrack-ready handling. With just 450 units produced from 1978-1981, its rarity and art connection (thanks to Andy Warhol) make it a true icon.

BMW M3 (E30) 1982

The E30 M3 is a cult classic and one of the best-selling M cars ever, combining a 2.3L four-cylinder engine producing 192 hp and lightweight design. Its motorsport pedigree and charm have created a dedicated fan base.

BMW M5 (E28) 1984

The E28 M5 was the world's first high-performance executive sedan, equipped with a 3.5L inline-six engine producing 282 hp. Its combination of luxury, technology, and speed set the standard for future M sedans.

BMW M6 (F13) 2012

The F13 M6 redefined the grand touring coupe. Its 4.4L twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces 560 hp, offering blistering performance combined with luxurious features and cutting-edge technology.

BMW X6 M (F86) 2015

The X6 M was a groundbreaking model in the performance SUV category. With a powerful 4.4L twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 567 hp, it combined the versatility of an SUV with the performance characteristics of a sports car, appealing to a new generation of drivers.

BMW M8 Competition (F91) 2019

The M8 Competition combines elegance with extreme performance. Its 4.4L V8 engine produces 617 hp, making it a blend of luxury and thrill, proving that the BMW M legacy continues into the future.
